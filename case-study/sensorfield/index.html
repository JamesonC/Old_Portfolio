Case Study

Sensorfield

An IoT manufacturer of wireless sensor devices and software that monitor oilfield equipment

The Role of a Pumper

A pumper is in charge of a x amount of oil and gas wells each day. A pumper’s route may take up to several driving hours every day to visit each one of these wells. They are responsible to make sure everything is working correctly, take oil tank gauges of production on a piece of paper, resolve any problems or spills, and then move on to the next. So the guys are essentially blind to what is happening across their fields until they are on-site. This is where remote monitoring benefits your operations.

Remote Monitoring & SCADA

Remote monitoring provides a 24 hour insight into what is going on across your fields. This leads to earlier detection of issues responsible for production delays, safety and environmental concerns and added expense. The norm for the past few decades have been SCADA systems. Only the mid tier and top tier companies have been able to justify the high costs of these systems. You have to lay wire, pour concrete, and have that company come out to install and configure everything.

Sensorfield - Simple Oilfield Monitoring

Sensorfield was founded in 2011, by Strode Pennebaker, a third-generation engineer and fourth-generation oilman. The company makes advanced remote oilfield monitoring solutions for wells that previously couldn’t justify any remote monitoring technology.

He wanted a simple, practical system to keep up with wells he operated himself. Something he could install and maintain himself with minimum bother.

He wanted a simple, practical system to keep up with wells he operated himself. Something he could install and maintain himself with minimum bother.

So he started with a basic goal: create a sensor the size of a refrigerator magnet, stick it onto a tank or pipe or anything else, and watch data show up on a desktop and cellphone display. This was a radical idea at the time - now it’s called the Internet of Things. And we’re the first to bring it to the oil patch.

They began by studying all of the commercial off-the-shelf sensors, radios and SCADA gear we could find. They quickly realized that they needed to redesign the entire system from the ground floor - otherwise they weren’t going to make the gear any more affordable than the next guy. And this certainly wouldn’t address the heart of the oil patch: the stripper well population.

So they started over and decided to custom-design and manufacture everything. They had the first system working in less than three months. But it took four more years to perfect the size, ruggedness and quality. Along the way we discovered a groundbreaking new RF technology and became the first company to write fully optimized software for it. They created new sensor designs and found novel ways to imbed high-resolution micro-sensors into rugged oilfield equipment. Then they optimized everything to run on micro-power from tiny solar panels less than 1–½ inches across.

Sensorfield flips the negatives associated with these SCADA systems, cost, installation, and size, turning them into positives. Below are the sensors the company has invented from the chip level up.

Sensors

  • Tank Level

  • Pressure Gauge

  • Activity Monitor

  • Beam Pump

They are so small we put them inside a medium sized Fedex box and ship Priority Overnight directly to the pumper’s doorstep. Simply place a wirelesss sensor on a tank, beam pump, compressor or other equipment you want to monitor. Super strong magnets will hold it in place. Push a button, and the sensor connects to your secure website and begins monitoring. The cost of the devices is drastically less than SCADA competitors installation fees alone.

Gateways

The gateway connects your sensors to the cloud and each other. Your tiny wireless gateway can connect to an unlimited number of sensors within its range, up to 1/2 mile in crowded areas, much more in open areas.

Hubs

Hubs boost the wireless signal and connect multiple gateways, sensors and other hubs. They increase the range by miles. Big field, no problem.

Target Market

There are roughly 1M oil and gas wells onshore in the United States, 50k of which are online monitoring, and 500k which need to be plugged or simply do not justify monitoring. Our target market are the other 450k “stripper” wells. We want to give the same capabilities the smaller operators that the mid and large oil and gas companies/operators have had for years.
